The hardest part of a project or a journey is not the project or the journey itself, but
the initiative to start — from 0 to 1.

People tend to wait until they are perfect before starting something. But we often forget:
we don't get perfect first and then do things. We get perfect *by doing things*.

Consider these familiar traps — *I need a proper mechanical keyboard before I can code
seriously. I need a bigger house with a garden before I start exercising.* Try reversing
them instead. Train yourself to think faster, then find a keyboard that keeps up with you.
Make yourself fit and eat well first, so you have the energy to earn that bigger house.

With that in mind, I started this blog.

## Perfectionist

This is probably the largest hinge holding back anyone who is great but not yet successful.
Nothing is perfect. I remind myself, in every aspect of life — make it work first, then
make it right along the way. Eventually it will be fast and stable, as I pictured it from
the start, or even beyond that.

## The Plan

I'm not sure what to write first, or even sure that in this AI era anyone would click into
a website and read it. But I'll use this as a small project — to learn, to deploy, and to
use as a notebook for things I've done that are worth sharing.
