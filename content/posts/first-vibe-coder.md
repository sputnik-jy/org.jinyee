## Introduction 

The term "Vibe Coding" can be date back to a [X post](https://x.com/karpathy/status/1886192184808149383?lang=en) (formerly Twitter) of the guy called Andrej Karpathy back in 3rd Feb, 2025, who is recognized as one of the founding member of OpenAI and the former director of AI at Tesla. In his own words from the original post, he coined the term as followed:

> There's a new kind of coding I call "vibe coding", where you fully give in to the vibes, embrace exponentials, and forget that the code even exists. 

Ever since then, the term has spawned endless discussion and sharing across the internet, on how programmers or even completely tech layman could use AI to vibe out their minimum viable product (MVP) over the weekends. Videos after videos showing how people use tools like Codex and superwhisper to do vibe coding, tutorials of setting up tools to do vibe coding.

## I think the first "Vibe Coder"

For those who doesn't know who's Michael Stonebraker, his creation is probably everywhere in our modern society and we don't even realise we're using it. According to the (ACM website)(https://amturing.acm.org/award_winners/stonebraker_1172121.cfm), he's the man behind the modern database system - _Postgres_. If you're a software developer of some sort, you probably already using it in your work or is going to use it soon.

The video mentioned, when developing Postgres in the UC Berkeley, he had never written a single code. He didn't need to code, instead he think. With his idea of making PostgreSQL an "extendable" database, allowing developers to add custom data types, functions, operators, and index methods without modifying core code, he leads a group of brilliant PhD students to help him out with the actual coding.

Then later, Michael Stonebraker left UC Berkeley to start another company. The story following in short is others PhD students accidentally take over and maintain the project and become the Postgres we have seen today. Postgres works and poliferated.


## The Moral of the Story

It's even more known since the AI boom and this [OpenAI](https://openai.com/index/scaling-postgresql/) post saying how they scale PostgreSQL. Maybe Michael never code a line or maybe the video is over exagerrated. The moral of the story however, is the in this era of generative AI, coding could become easy that it's ever been. However, the *idea*, the *vision*, the *creativity*, the Eureka moment of what defines us as a human being, so far IMHO is still far long to be replace by AI.

That's why, as the topic of this post, vibe coding is not something new, anyone could ask someone good at coding to reliase their amazing idea. Now that someone is a giant, sophisticated code repeater - _the  AI_. The sad thing is, most of the so called "ideas" today are nothing new or game changing, most of us just want to get the CRUD monkey job done, or invent another wheel that your superior trying to innovate. Most of the time, after the vibe coder has vibe out the code, all they will do is pick up their phone and start scrolling tixxxxk and inst___m, without even trying to understand or learn about the great things that _the AI_ just copied from.

And if one day AI is easy to use enough so that anyone can replicate those products by exploiting (or stealing from Github w/o consent) the publicly available source code, what's the intrinsic value as a software engineer / programmer / engineer in general. And if that day comes, maybe the question isn't what's the value of a software engineer — but rather, what kind of human do you want to be? One who understands the machine, or one who is slowly becoming indistinguishable from it?
